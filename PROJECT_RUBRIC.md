## Outcome One: Media - Communication (30 points)

**Evaluation Criteria:**

- [ ] **Information Architecture (5 pts)**
  - Content is well-organized and easy to navigate
  - Clear hierarchy of information (hero → features → modules → resources)
  - Navigation is intuitive for elderly users and multi-language speakers

- [ ] **Visual Design & Presentation (8 pts)**
  - Design is professional and polished, not amateur
  - Angular aesthetic with sharp clip-path elements (not rounded)
  - Warm color palette bleeds throughout (reds, golds, creams)
  - Visual hierarchy is clear (headings, spacing, contrast)
  - Mobile-responsive design works on all devices

- [ ] **Written Communication (5 pts)**
  - Text is clear, concise, and accessible
  - Language is appropriate for target audience (adults, elderly, non-native speakers)
  - No jargon without explanation
  - Tone is professional but friendly

- [ ] **Multimedia Integration (5 pts)**
  - Slideshow presentations are embedded/accessible
  - QR codes function correctly and link to appropriate content
  - Visual elements (icons, images, diagrams) enhance understanding
  - Images are high-quality and relevant (e.g., Chinatown photo)

- [ ] **Engagement & Interactivity (7 pts)**
  - Module cards are clickable and modals function smoothly
  - FAQ search and filters work correctly
  - Language selector functions properly
  - Call-to-action buttons are prominent and functional
  - Overall user experience is smooth and intuitive

## Outcome Two: World Language - Transfer (30 points)

**Evaluation Criteria:**

- [ ] **Mandarin Chinese Proficiency (8 pts)**
  - Simplified Chinese translation is accurate and natural
  - Traditional Chinese translation is accurate and natural
  - Terminology is appropriate for civic/legal context
  - Tone matches English version

- [ ] **Language Application in Real Context (8 pts)**
  - Language is used effectively to communicate civic information
  - Translations make content accessible to non-English speakers
  - Proper nouns and legal terms are handled correctly across languages
  - Cultural nuances are respected in translation

- [ ] **Trilingual Functionality (7 pts)**
  - Language switcher works seamlessly
  - All major content is translated (not just partial)
  - Language preference persists (cookie-based)
  - No broken text or untranslated sections

- [ ] **Accessibility Across Languages (7 pts)**
  - All three languages are equally prominent and respected
  - User can navigate entire site in chosen language
  - Font/character display is correct for Chinese (no encoding issues)
  - Logical flow is maintained in all three languages

## Outcome Three: Cultural History - Critical Thinking (40 points)

**Evaluation Criteria:**

- [ ] **Historical & Cultural Context (8 pts)**
  - Content demonstrates understanding of Chinese American immigration history
  - References to Executive Order 9066, deportation concerns are accurate
  - Awareness of contemporary civic challenges facing immigrant communities
  - Content is informed by real community needs and conversations

- [ ] **Accuracy & Research Quality (8 pts)**
  - Information is factually correct and verified
  - Content is based on credible sources (ACLU, legal experts, community orgs)
  - Attorney reviewers are named and credited
  - Sources are cited appropriately
  - "Last updated" dates are current

- [ ] **Critical Analysis (8 pts)**
  - Content goes beyond surface-level information
  - Explains WHY rights matter, not just WHAT they are
  - Acknowledges complexity and gray areas
  - Helps users understand systems of power and governance
  - Connects individual rights to community impact

- [ ] **Unbiased & Inclusive Presentation (8 pts)**
  - Information is presented objectively, not propagandistically
  - Respects different perspectives while maintaining accuracy
  - Avoids stereotyping any group
  - Accessible to diverse literacy levels
  - Language is empowering, not patronizing

- [ ] **Credibility & Expert Backing (8 pts)**
  - Attorney names are visible in credibility boxes
  - Partner organizations are prominently featured
  - Disclaimer is clear: "Expert-informed, not legal advice"
  - Trust signals are present (credentials, sourcing, dates)
  - User confidence in information is high
